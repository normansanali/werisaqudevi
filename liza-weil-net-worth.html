Liza Weil Wiki Biography

Liza Weil was born on the 5th June 1977 in Passaic, New Jersey, USA of Jewish origin, and is an actress perhaps best known for her role of Paris Geller in the television series “Gilmore Girls” (2000 – 2007) and that of Bonnie Winterbottom in the television series “How to Get Away with Murder” (2014 – present). Weil has been active in the entertainment industry since 1983.

How rich is the actress? It has been reported by authoritative sources that the outright size of Liza Weil’s net worth is as much as $3 million, as of the data presented in early 2017. Films and television are the major sources of Weil’s modest fortune.

To begin with, she spent much of her childhood travelling through Europe with the comedy company of her parents. Because she grew up in an atmosphere of action, it was no surprise that she developed an interest for acting. Eventually, the family settled down in Lansdale, Pennsylvania, USA, but she began commuting to New York for professional auditions. She started on stage, performed off-Broadway in New York and in regional productions in Philadelphia.

Liza Weil made her film debut with the starring role of Anna Stockard in the drama film “Whatever” (1998). She then appeared in the thriller film “Stir of Echoes” (1999) with Kevin Bacon, and the independent drama “My Baby’s Gone” (2000). Subsequently, Liza decided to move to Los Angeles which put Weil’s television career in motion. She quickly gained guest roles in the television series “The West Wing” (2000) and “ER” (2000), in which she played the wife of a schizophrenic. She also had a guest role in an episode of “Law & Order: Special Victims Unit” (2001) as a rape victim, and then played the role of Paris Geller in the television series “The Gilmore Girls” (2000 – 2007). In 2008, Liza was cast as a main in the comedy drama film “The Missing Person” directed by Noah Buschel, followed by a starring role in the psychological slasher film “Smiley” (2012) by Michael Gallagher. The same year, she had the recurring role of Amanda Tanner in the political thriller series “Scandal” created by Shonda Rhimes. In 2013, the actress landed another recurring role in the comedy drama series “Bunheads”, aired on the ABC Family channel. Her most recent role is that of Bonnie Winterbottom in the television series created by Peter Nowalk – “How to Get Away with Murder” (2014 – present) – broadcast on the ABC.

To conclude, all the above mentioned roles have added sums to the total size of Liza Weil’s net worth; more, the roles have certainly made her popular.

Finally, in the personal life of the actress, Liza married the American actor Paul Adelstein at the end of 2006; the two had met on the “Order Up” (2006) film set. In the spring of 2010, their daughter named Josephine Elizabeth Weil Adelstein was born. However, the couple announced their divorce in the spring of 2016, and currently Liza claims to be single.
