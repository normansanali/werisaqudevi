Elizabeth Banks splashes out $6.85 million on a lavish five-bedroom property

 Posted on August 25, 2024  |  8 minutes  |  1591 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Hollywood star Elizabeth Banks has spent $6.85 million on a lavish new property in the San Fernando Valley, a companion to her comparatively modest home in Los Angeles neighbourhood Studio City. 

Spread over 6,572 square-feet, the Pitch Perfect star's single-story ranch-style home offers sweeping views of Sherman Oaks and the distant Los Angeles sprawl from every window. 

Private gates lead on to a neatly manicured lawn, strategically placed sun-loungers and a sizable outdoor swimming pool, with its vantage point offering further unobstructed views of the city's twinkling downtown lights.

Generously sized terraces offer Elizabeth and TV producer husband Max Handelman plenty of room to entertain guests, while 1.18 acres of rolling grasslands lead down to the valley, above which the property is set.

Inside, the property embraces a distinctly European theme, with French hardwood floors running throughout each of its open plan kitchen, dining room, five bedrooms and five accompanying bathrooms. 

Arched wooden ceilings with exposed beams and modest skylights create a feeling of intimacy, while three antique French stone fireplaces prove to be striking focal points. 

Come on in: The property is accessible through an enormous set of wooden security gates 

Fancy a swim? The gates lead guests onto a neatly manicured lawn, strategically placed sun-loungers and a sizable outdoor swimming pool

Welcome: A generously sized patio offers Pitch Perfect star  Elizabeth plenty of room to entertain guests

Taking further advantage of its stunning views, the property's master bedroom suite opens onto its own patio, another feature in many of its large, tastefully decorated rooms. 

A huge bathroom features a large roll-top bath, a bank of marble sinks and a glass panelled shower, with obligatory patio doors creating further light and space to a property that already has an abundance of both.

Built in 1941 and originally owned by local advertising executive Benedict Coulter, the house also boasts slate roofs and a complete solar energy system.

Coulter reportedly listed the home for $6,995,000 in December 2015, almost two-and-a-half years before Banks and Handelman purchased for a fraction less than its original asking price. 

The couple already own a mid-century property in Studio City, where they have lived since 2007. 

According to  property records the comparatively modest four bedroom,  3,200-square-foot home was bought for $1,625,000.

Banks has previously compared the buying and selling of houses to gambling, telling Architectural Digest: 'I have bought three homes, four homes maybe. So, that's something that I really enjoy doing. 

'My husband is obsessed with real estate and we live in California, which has got a really interesting real-estate situation, in that there is sort of a housing crisis here and the median home price is insane.' 

She added: 'I just find that real estate, it's just like the racetrack. Something I invest a little bit of my money in.'

Revealing a keen interest in interior design, the actress admitted she prefers to rely on neutral colours while injecting a splash of vibrancy into furniture and accessories.

'I have never done a total teardown, so I am always looking for great bones in a home, and I like spaces where I understand how to live,' she said. 

'If I walk into a place and I can feel how I am going to entertain my girlfriends for wine and cheese on a Friday night, then that feels like a home to me.' 

Wash and go: A huge bathroom features a large  free standing bath and a glass panelled shower, with obligatory floor to ceiling windows creating further light and space to a property that already has an abundance of both

Cleaning up: A marble wash basin and toilet sit adjacent to a modest shower in another of the property's bathrooms

Banks married producer Handelman, with whom she runs Brownstone Productions, in 2003 after meeting at the University of Pennsylvania, but she previously admitted it wasn't exactly love at first sight. 

'I didn’t meet my husband and think, I’ve met the man I’m going to marry,” she told Allure. 'I was like, "He’s cute. I’ll f**k him, because I’m 18 and in college.”'  

She added: 'Really, what happened was I’ve never met anyone that I liked more. In the early years, did I have crushes or little interests here and there? I know my husband did, and so did I, but we stayed together.'
