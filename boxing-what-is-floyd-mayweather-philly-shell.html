How often did Floyd Mayweather use ‘Philly Shell’?

T.B.E. didn’t come up with the Philly Shell or the shoulder roll. Mayweather effectively utilized it for his fights. The Philly Shell is one of boxing’s most brilliant defensive strategies. It allows a boxer to neutralize his opponent’s jabs completely. Whenever a boxer threw a jab, the opponent’s shoulder came up.

Hence, the jab either slides off or bounces off the shoulder, never reaching the intended target. Floyd Mayweather honed this technique by adding his own reflexes and counter-punching, making it unstoppable. The Philly Shell helped Mayweather to effectively combat some of the best boxers like Oscar De La Hoya, Manny Pacquiao, and Canelo Alvarez. He neutralized their offensive style with his defensive prowess.

The Philly Shell was used before Floyd Mayweather in boxing

The ‘Philly Shell‘ is mostly thought to have been initially used in the Philadelphia boxing gymnasiums, hence the name. One of the most famous fighting cities in the world is Philadelphia, and it has always had an endless supply of raw talent. Furthermore, with fighters like  George Benton, Joe Frazier, and Bernard Hopkins, it’s clear that Philadelphia has produced some of the greatest boxers ever.

Watching any of Hopkins’ later fights demonstrated the design and usage of the Philly Shell in a fight or sparring session. In the late rounds of the fight, Hopkins would “Shell Up” to the ropes to get his opponent’s attention. Subsequently, if they got close enough, they would be afraid of the counter. The counter banked from the Philly Shell could prove deadly. However, Mayweather forged an undefeated career with Philly Shell.
