Who Was Dede Robertson? Wikipedia Bio

Dede Robertson, also known as Adelia Elmer, was Pat Robertson’s wife, according to Wikipedia.

She earned her bachelor’s degree in nursing at Ohio State University and continued her education at Yale, where she met her spouse. She was a nurse who dabbled in writing.

Similarly, she was a beauty queen in the Miss Ohio State Pageant and worked as a fashion model. Robertson has founded a number of notable organizations, including the Christian Broadcasting Network, International Family Entertainment Inc, American Center for Law & Justice, Founders Inn and Conference Center, Christian Coalition, and others.

Dede was a published author who published two books, My God Will Provide and The New You. She also used to write All in a Woman’s Day, a monthly column for Christian Life.

She was also named the United States’ principal delegate to the Inter-American Commission on Women in 1982. She then traveled to all IACW meetings, including the General Assembly, to represent the United States.

Dede Robertson took her last breath in Virginia on April 19, 2022 according to the CBN website.
