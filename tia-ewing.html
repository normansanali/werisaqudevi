Career and Professional Life

Tia Ewing began her career in Flint Michigan. She was able to work as an anchor and reporter at WNEM. She also was able to cover investigative stories surrounding an unethical city home building project, the vicious killing, and murder case of 4-year-old Dominick Calhoun, and former Vice President Joe Biden as well.

Likewise, she then moved to KTXL in Sacramento, California, as a reporter. Soon after, she was able to be promoted to being an anchor as well as a reporter. She nearly was able to spend her last year at KTXL Fox 40 as an evening anchor. Moreover, Ewing was able to join WOIO in Cleveland, Ohio, in the year 2015 as a morning anchor.

During her time there, she was nominated for a NATAS Lower Great Lakes Chapter Emmy for Team Coverage for the Republican National Convention. And, one of the most important and highlighted moments of her career was taking the floor of Quicken Loans Arena during the Cleveland Cavaleir’s half-time show to present a speech honoring the life of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.

Furthermore, Tia Ewing is also a member of the National Association of Black Journalists and is currently working at FOX 32 where she works alongside other popular FOX 32 anchors.
