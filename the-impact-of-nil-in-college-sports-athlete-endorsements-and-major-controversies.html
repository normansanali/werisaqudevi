The Impact of NIL in College Sports: Athlete Endorsements and MAJOR Controversies

 Posted on July 26, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  631 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Over the last few years, a sea change has swept through the world of college athletics in the form of Name, Image, and Likeness rights for student-athletes. This revolutionary move not only created new avenues for athletes but also ignited a firestorm of controversies and issues that have since altered the landscape of college sports.


The Emergence of NIL Rights


NIL has given student-athletes a first-of-its-kind chance to cash in on their brands via endorsements, sponsorships, and more. However, having this newfound empowerment the athletes can take advantage of their skill and tremendous effort and make that happen while most importantly being able to go to school and run track.
NIL deals serve as an umbrella term for a variety of opportunities such as meet and greets, brand endorsements, media appearances, etc. Caitlin Clark, Bronny James, Livvy Dunne, Shedeur Sanders, and Arch Manning are among the athletes who have cashed in on these opportunities with big strikeouts, inking seven-figure deals and becoming brand partners for major corporations.


Impact on College Athletics


NIL rights have not only emboldened student-athletes but have presented challenges for universities, sports organizations, and the sports industry overall. This is where colleges and universities become the single point of control which provides needed support and counsel to the student-athletes to be certain that they are correctly informed and that they adhere to the rules. The need for educational initiatives in financial literacy and personal branding to help student-athletes succeed and navigate the NIL era and beyond has been magnified.
Yet, the implementation of NIL rights has also not entirely gone free of controversy. Donor-funded collectives have been established to assist student-athletes through the intricacies of the business world, leading to fears over possible recruiting inducements and the role of schools in hooking up these athletes with endorsement deals. Because the NCAA banned its member schools from paying their athletes until this week, much less contracting them for NIL deals, third-party collectives have arisen and started paying athletes to bring them in, creating all sorts of ethical and regulatory headaches.


Recent Legal and Legislative Activities


Expansion of the NIL rights has been a major story in the legal landscape. Virginia signed a bill into law allowing universities and schools to pay their student-athletes directly for their NIL, a direct defiance of NCAA's rules. The legislation has the potential to have a big impact on college athletics, giving schools in Virginia more flexibility in how they can go after revenue for student-athletes and maybe even recruiting battles.
The NCAA has also tried to build NIL consumer protection rules, such as student-athletes needing to disclose any payment of more than $600 from an NIL deal reported to their schools. Additionally, the NCAA has proposed that colleges be granted greater oversight and authority in NIL deals for their student-athletes, hoping that this will help to ameliorate the NIL landscape.


College Sports: The Next Generation


There is little doubt that one thing is for certain as the future comes to pass we will not see the same face of college athletics that it once was. The potential impact of NIL rights upon collegiate sports is novel and unprecedented, on the one hand transforming collegiate athletics by providing new opportunities for student-athletes, and on the other presenting university athletic departments with new challenges. To habituate a sustainable NIL ecosystem in College Sports it requires  collaboration, innovation, respect, land leadership.
The introduction of NIL rights has opened a new era of opportunity for student-athletes — however, it has also raised complex legal, ethical, and regulatory considerations. While the NIL era soldiers on within the collegiate sports community, the presence of clear transparency, educational measures and a code of ethics has to be at the forefront of everyone's mind to ensure an equitable playing field for all parties concerned.
