Who is David Copperfield? | David Copperfield Biography and Wiki

David Copperfield is an American professional magician regarded as the most commercially successful magician in history. He played the character The Magician in the 1980 horror film Terror Train and had an uncredited appearance in the 1994 film Prêt-à-Porter.

David Copperfield Magic Show

Copperfield began hosting ‘The Magic of ABC’ on ABC which was produced by Joseph Cates who is also the person who discovered David in 1977. He also presented ‘ The Magic of David Copperfield’ specials on CBS between 1978 and 2001. Between 7th September 1977 and 3rd April 2001, there have been 17 Copperfield TV specials and 2 documentaries.

Some of David’s illusions include; the disappearance of a Learjet (1981), the vanishing and reappearance of the Statue of Liberty (1983), levitating over the Grand Canyon (1984), walking through the Great Wall of China (1986), escaping from Alcatraz prison (1987), the disappearance of an Orient Express dining car (1991) and flying on stage for several minutes (1992).

On 8th April 1983, he performed his most famous illusion when he made the Statue of Liberty disappear and reappear. To give the aerial view of the illusion a helicopter hovered overhead with only the circle of lights surrounding it still present and visible. The illusion was filmed in the long take to demonstrate the absence of camera tricks.

In 1996 his Broadway show ‘ Dreams & Nightmares’ which was in collaboration with Francis Ford Coppola, David Ives, and Eiko Ishioka broke box office records in New York at the Martin Beck Theatre. He gave the audience a mind-blogging performance with his disappearance and reappearance, making the audience vanish and others levitate and also cutting himself to two. He climaxes his show with a flying routine that defies both logic and visual evidence.
