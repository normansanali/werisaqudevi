Meet Garry Kissick, Ashleigh Barty Partner

Ashleigh Barty and her boyfriend turned fiance Garry Kissick met each other in 2016. The world champion tennis player met her partner at Brooklands Golf Club in Queensland when she was playing a round of golf. The pair clicked with each other instantaneously and have been in a relationship ever since.

The pair went public with their relationship in 2017 while attending the John Newcombe Awards together. “He’s extremely patient with me, and when we met he didn’t know a lot about tennis,” Ashleigh opened up during her interview with Vogue Australia.

“He’s kind of been thrown in the very deep end in understanding what the tour is like and how much we’re apart… he’s the best person to have around in the sense of switching off from tennis and being able to bring the fun and laughter when we are training and not be so serious all the time.”

Garry had announced publicly that Ashleigh was “the best person I’ve ever met”.

An insider revealed, “They’re the most normal couple you’ll find at that level in tennis.” “It’s refreshing because you often see mismatched liaisons in this sport, with some partners unable to cope with the public attention always focused on the person they love and not on them.”

The insider also revealed that “Without even meaning to, Garry helps Ash play her very best tennis, because there’s certainly no pressure from him.”

Garry proposed to Ashleigh on 15 November 2021. He announced the engagement via IG post where he wrote, “15/11/2021 I love you @ashbarty ❤”. They kept their engagement private for about a week before both of them publicly announced via their socials.

Whereas Ashleigh shared about her engagement, “Garry and I have been together for a long time now and I had designed the ring with him. We are excited now for the next chapter. We were at home on the couch with the puppies. It was very much just us. It was perfect for us.”

Ashleigh also went on her IG and captioned the picture: “Future Husband,” alongside a ring and heart emoji.

In another post, Garry wrote on his IG, “You amaze me, every single day! So incredibly proud of you babe”.

Garry Kissick Age

At the time of this writing in late January 2022, Garry Kissick was 30 years old.

What Does Garry Kissick Do?

On Garry Kissick’s LinkedIn bio he wrote, “I have been a golfer all my life so working in the golf industry has always been my passion. I’ve been working at Brookwater Golf Club for over 12 years and I am currently the Trainee Professional under John Collins after spending 10 years on the maintenance team.”

He added further, “I have worked with some very knowledgeable superintendents over the past 10 years and they have given me confidence in my abilities as an agronomist and my skill sets in the workplace and day-to-day life.”

Garry started his career in Brookwater Golf & Country Club as Golf Operations when he joined in December 2005. He worked there for two years in that role until November 2007. Then, he became Apprentice Greenkeeper from November 2007 to November 2010. He completed tasks as delegated by the Course Superintendent and Assistant Course Superintendent Assist in day to day maintenance of the course.

Starting from November 2010, Garry started working as a Qualified Greenkeeper. He kept working in the position till January 2014.

Currently, Garry has been working as Irrigation Technician and PGA Trainee Professional at Brookwater Golf & Country Club.

Speaking of his academic qualifications, Garry graduated from Ipswich Grammar school in 2007. After that, he attended Brisbane North Institute of TAFE to study Applied Horticulture/Horticulture Operations and earned his certificate in 2010.

He also worked as a caddie for Louis Dobbelaar during Fortinet Australian PGA Championship.
