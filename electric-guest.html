Overall Meaning

The song "Troubleman" by Electric Guest is a melancholic yet captivating tune that speaks of love, growth, and moving on. The first part of the lyrics depicts a girl who was once young, bohemian, and full of life. The singer, perhaps someone who knows her intimately, watched her grow and eventually gained wisdom. The lines "Me and my heart, following the shadow she made," suggest that the singer may have pursued her, chasing her like the sun, but failed to keep up with her. As time passed, she became someone new, and the singer realized he broke the spell and got over her.


The second part of the lyrics mentions a "Troubleman" who wished it all away and found a path with a gun in hand. The reference to the "Troubleman" is vague and open to interpretation, but it could mean someone who sought trouble, or someone who had a difficult life. The lines "He's gotten bad for me, he's gotten bad for me," could refer back to the girl in the first part, emphasizing that the singer has moved on from her and into someone new. The repeated line "Oh, no. You go home, we go home," could mean that it's time to put the past behind us and move forward.


Overall, "Troubleman" is a poignant song that explores the complexities of love and growth. It's a song that speaks of overcoming obstacles and learning to let go of the past.
