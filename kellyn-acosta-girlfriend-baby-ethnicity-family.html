What Is Kellyn Acosta Ethnicity?

Kellyn belongs to mixed ethnicity because of his family’s diverse ethnic background. His paternal grandmother is Japanese and his father was born in Japan.

Moreover, his mother is Puerto Rican. His paternal step-grandfather is Mexican and his surname Acosta comes from him.

Acosta was eligible for Japan or the United States but chose to represent the United States.

His Career Journey

Kellyn spent his youth at FC Dallas Development Academy in his hometown. Later, he committed to play soccer at the University of Maryland.

In August 2013, he made his first-team debut for FC Dallas. Initially, he played as a fullback and eventually transitioned to a defensive midfielder.

Acosta was traded to Colorado Rapids on 23 July 2018. He spent three seasons there and was acquired by Los Angeles FC on 14 January 2022.


Internationally, he has played in the 2011 FIFA U-17 World Cup and became the youngest member of the U.S. squad at the 2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup.  On 31 January 2016, he made his senior international debut for the United States in a friendly game against Iceland.
